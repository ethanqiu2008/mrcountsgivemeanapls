Biography

An in-depth look at his early life, career, and presidency

Ronald Wilson Reagan (1911–2004) was an American politician, actor, and the 40th President of the United States. Known as "The Great Communicator," he captivated millions with his charisma and leadership.

Before his presidency, Reagan enjoyed a flourishing career in Hollywood and later served as the Governor of California. His presidency was characterized by bold economic reforms—dubbed "Reaganomics"—and a steadfast approach during the Cold War. His legacy is one of transforming political discourse in America.
