Ronald Reagan

40th President of the United States (1981–1989)

"Government's first duty is to protect the people, not run their lives." - Ronald Reagan

Biography

Ronald Wilson Reagan (1911–2004) was an American politician and actor who served as the 40th president of the United States from 1981 to 1989. Before his presidency, he was a Hollywood actor and the Governor of California (1967–1975).

Reagan's presidency was marked by conservative economic policies, the end of the Cold War, and a strong stance against the Soviet Union. His famous "Reaganomics" policy emphasized tax cuts and deregulation.

Key Achievements

End of the Cold War: Played a significant role in the fall of the Soviet Union.

Economic Policies: Introduced Reaganomics, which reduced taxes and stimulated growth.

Great Communicator: Famous for speeches like "Mr. Gorbachev, tear down this wall!"
