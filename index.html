Ronald Reagan

40th President of the United States (1981–1989)

"Government's first duty is to protect the people, not run their lives."

Biography

Ronald Wilson Reagan (1911–2004) was an American politician, actor, and the 40th president of the United States. Before his presidency, he served as the Governor of California and was a well-known Hollywood actor.

Reagan's leadership was marked by conservative economic policies, famously dubbed "Reaganomics", a strategic approach during the Cold War, and a charismatic style that earned him the title "The Great Communicator." His policies and public speeches remain influential in American politics.

Key Achievements

End of the Cold War

Played a pivotal role in reducing Cold War tensions, leading to the eventual fall of the Soviet Union.

Reaganomics

Implemented significant tax cuts and deregulation, influencing economic policies for decades.

Great Communicator

Famous for his inspirational speeches and persuasive public communication.
